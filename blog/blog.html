Bonsai

Introduction

The word “Bon-sai” (often misspelled as bonzai or banzai) is a Japanese term which, literally translated, means “planted in a container”. This art form is derived from an ancient Chinese horticultural practice, part of which was then redeveloped under the influence of Japanese Zen Buddhism.Techniques such as pinching buds, pruning and wiring branches, and carefully restricting but not abandoning fertilizers are used to limit and redirect healthy growth. Most commonly kept under four feet (or about a meter) in height, Bonsai are not genetically dwarfed plants. However, plants with smaller leaves do make these compositions easier to design.

Benefits Of Bonsai

You already know that bonsai plants enhance the décor in your home or the terrace. But there is more to a bonsai plant. Bonsai plants benefit the owner or gardener’s psyche. Bonsai plants need a lot of care and time. You would learn to be loving and caring. You would also practice patience. Develop the traits of consistency, confidence, self-control and self-awareness. Explore your creative side as you style the bonsai plants to their spectacular look.Some other bonsai plant benefits are that they are good for the atmosphere in your home. If you have a bonsai tree indoors, it purifies the air inside your house by releasing oxygen. Having bonsai plants outdoors has similar benefits along with providing a green zone to de-stress or meditate in. A bonsai tree indoor can reduce the frequency of dry skin, sore throats and cold as they maintain the humidity in the atmosphere. They are great gifting options. Finally, need a lifelong friend? Grow a bonsai. Bonsai plants are known to have lived through generations making them family heirlooms as well.

Demerits Of Bonsai

The major disadvantages of bonsai plants are that they require constant care and attention, need watering daily, can be expensive, and have been linked with increased insect infestations. Bonsai plants cannot be left for weeks on end by themselves when compared to other plant hobbies. Bonsais require a lot of work in other areas such as watering which needs to be done daily or even twice daily in some instances. Also, be prepared for a lot of trial and error when you get started with your first few designs not likely to look very good especially if you are learning bonsai by yourself – it’s not uncommon to spend hours trying to trim your first bonsai only for it to look like a Frankenstein version of the design you had in your head. On top of this, the journey is never complete and your bonsai will never be at a stage where you will be happy with it – part of the charm is that your project will never be finished so be prepared to invest a lot of time and hours in your new-found hobby – which is definitely concerning if you don’t have a lot of free time!
